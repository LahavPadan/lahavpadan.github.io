identical features

One fitted contribution, many coefficient pairs

step 1 · derive the constraint

Assume that the two columns of the design matrix are identical.

$\displaystyle \mathbf{x}_1=\mathbf{x}_2=\mathbf{x} \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \beta_1\mathbf{x}_1+\beta_2\mathbf{x}_2 =(\beta_1+\beta_2)\mathbf{x}.$

Fix the combined coefficient $c$. The fitted contribution is $c\mathbf{x}$ precisely for coefficient pairs satisfying $\beta_1+\beta_2=c$.

Move along $\beta_1+\beta_2=c$

The control sets $\beta_1=\alpha c$ and $\beta_2=(1-\alpha)c$. Their sum remains $c$, so every slider position gives the same fitted contribution.

$\ell_1$

Every nonnegative split reaches the same lower bound

The triangle inequality supplies a lower bound for every point on the constraint line.

$\beta_1$ 72% of total
$\beta_2$ 28% of total
$\beta_1+\beta_2=c$
smallest $\ell_1$ level set
current split

For every pair with $\beta_1+\beta_2=c$ and $c>0$,

$\displaystyle |\beta_1|+|\beta_2| \ge |\beta_1+\beta_2|=c.$

On the segment $0\leq\beta_1\leq c$ with $\beta_2=c-\beta_1\geq0$, the absolute values disappear and the penalty equals $c$ exactly. Therefore every point on that segment is an $\ell_1$ minimizer.

$\ell_2$

Completing the square leaves one possible minimizer

Substitute the constraint into the squared penalty and separate its constant part.

$\beta_1$ 50% of total
$\beta_2$ 50% of total
$\beta_1+\beta_2=c$
smallest $\ell_2$ level set
$\beta_1=\beta_2=c/2$

Using $\beta_2=c-\beta_1$,

$\displaystyle \beta_1^2+\beta_2^2 =\beta_1^2+(c-\beta_1)^2 =\frac{c^2}{2}+2\left(\beta_1-\frac{c}{2}\right)^2.$

The second term is nonnegative and becomes zero only at $\beta_1=c/2$. The constraint then gives $\beta_2=c/2$. The $\ell_2$ minimizer is the unique equal split.

→

The data determine only $\beta_1+\beta_2=c$. The $\ell_1$ penalty is constant on the entire nonnegative segment, whereas the $\ell_2$ penalty has the single minimizer $(c/2,c/2)$.
