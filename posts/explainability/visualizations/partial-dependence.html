Partial dependence plot

Hold one feature fixed, reuse every observed context, then average the model outputs.

A PDP is not obtained by selecting the training rows whose feature already equals a chosen value. It creates a synthetic copy of every row, overwrites only feature xj, evaluates the model, and averages those predictions.

PDj(z) = 1/n ∑i=1n f(z, x−j(i)) The average is over the empirical marginal distribution of all other features.
01 Keep the contexts Retain each observed x−j(i).
02 Overwrite one feature Set xj = z in every copied row.
03 Evaluate the model Compute f(z, x−j(i)) for every context.
04 Average vertically One mean prediction becomes one point of the PDP.

What a changing PDP means

The model’s average prediction changes when xj is varied while the empirical distribution of the remaining features is reused.

What it does not mean

It does not say that every individual row responds in the same way, and it does not preserve dependence between xj and x−j.

The interaction from the article
f(X1, X2, X3) = 0.2X1 − 5X2 + 10X21[X3 > 0]

For X3 ≤ 0, increasing X2 pushes the prediction downward. For X3 > 0, it pushes the prediction upward by the same magnitude.

Two strong conditional effects, one average

The PDP line is the weighted pointwise average of the two conditional response lines.

PDP slope = 0
X3 ≤ 0: slope −5 X3 > 0: slope +5 PDP average
The cancellation in algebra

A flat PDP can be produced by opposing effects, not by the absence of an effect.

when X₃ ≤ 0 f2(x2 | X3 ≤ 0) = −5x2
when X₃ > 0 f2(x2 | X3 > 0) = +5x2
PD2(x2) = (1 − π)(−5x2) + π(5x2) = 0 · x2 = 0
At π = 0.50, the PDP is exactly flat.

Both subgroups have a large effect of magnitude 5, but the marginal average reports zero variation.

The diagnostic implication: compare the PDP with individual conditional expectation curves or stratified PDPs. A single average curve cannot reveal heterogeneous or sign-changing effects by itself.
