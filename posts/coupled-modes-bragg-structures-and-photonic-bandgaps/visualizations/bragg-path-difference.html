Interference

Constructive reflection means a whole number of wavelengths

The lower ray may travel farther, but it reinforces the upper ray whenever the added distance contains m = 1, 2, 3, … complete wavelengths.

m λmedium = ΔL = 2Λ sin θ

Using λ0 = navg λmedium, the same condition becomes m λ0 = 2 navg Λ sin θ.
What this picture answers

Where constructive reflection occurs

It connects the plane spacing, incidence angle, wavelength, and diffraction order through one geometric path difference.

What it deliberately leaves open

How strong and how wide the reflection is

A discrete-plane picture does not determine the strength of a Fourier order or the width of the resulting stopband; the later derivations supply those pieces.
