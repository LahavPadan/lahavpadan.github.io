Step 1 · cavity modes without magnetic bias

From one cavity mode to the three port responses

The circular cavity sets the standing-wave shape. The three waveguides then read that same field at the three angles where they meet the disk.

Ψm(r, φ) = J|m|(kr) cos(mφ)
Choose the real standing wave whose antinode is aligned with port 1. Because all three ports touch the cavity at the same radius r = R, the radial factor is common to all three. Therefore the relative port fields come only from Em(φ) = cos(mφ).

Interpret what the three field values mean

Equal magnitudes at P2 and P3

The dipole gives E(P2) = E(P3) = −1/2. The minus sign means that the local oscillating field is reversed in phase relative to P1; it does not mean negative energy. Coupled power depends on |E|², so P2 and P3 receive equal power.

Where m = +1 and m = −1 enter

The drawn dipole is a real standing wave, not a third independent mode. It is the equal superposition of the two counter-rotating cavity modes.

cos φ = ½(eiφ + e−iφ)

Without magnetic bias these two modes have the same resonance frequency, so neither circulation direction is preferred. The result is the stationary dipole above and symmetric coupling toward P2 and P3.

m = 0

Breathing mode

No angular variation: E(φ) = 1, so the three ports read (1, 1, 1).

m = ±1

Dipole doublet

One nodal diameter. A P1-aligned dipole gives (1, −1/2, −1/2), hence equal coupling toward P2 and P3.

m = ±2

Quadrupole doublet

Two nodal diameters. The same three point values recur, so three samples alone do not distinguish it from the dipole.
