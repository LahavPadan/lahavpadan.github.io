Gyromagnetic media · § 5.1

A magnetic field turns the moment without changing its length

The torque is always sideways. That one geometric fact produces precession, sets the Larmor rate, and survives unchanged when many microscopic moments are combined into a magnetization.

Equation of motion dμ/dt = γ μ × B
magnetic momentIts length stays fixed while its direction changes.
static bias fieldThe field supplies the axis around which the motion turns.
instantaneous changeThe derivative is tangent to the orbit, never along the moment.

The animation is slowed to a visible rate. Its speed is proportional to |γB₀|; changing the sign of γ reverses the handedness.

Nothing pulls the moment inward or outward. The field only supplies a perpendicular change at every instant.

1

The field produces a sideways torque

A cross product is perpendicular to both inputs, so the change cannot point along μ.

μ · dμ/dt = γ μ · (μ × B) = 0
no component of the change can lengthen or shorten μ
2

Therefore the magnitude is conserved

Only the direction can move. Repeating that infinitesimal sideways turn traces a circle, and the full vector traces a cone.

d|μ|²/dt = 2μ · dμ/dt = 0
the motion is rotation, not oscillation in length
3

The bias fixes the turning rate

For B = B₀ ẑ, the transverse part rotates uniformly around the field axis.

|ω₀| = |γ|B₀
for an electron at 0.70 T: |ω₀|/2π ≈ 19.6 GHz
1st
First-order motion The present direction determines the next direction directly. No separate “angular-momentum velocity” is needed.

The same precession law at three scales

nothing new is assumed when moving from one moment to the material response

angular momentum
dL/dt = τ,   τ = μ × B
Torque changes the direction of angular momentum.
→
one magnetic moment
μ = γL   ⇒   dμ/dt = γμ × B
The magnetic moment is proportional to the same rotating vector.
→
many moments per volume
M = Σμ/V   ⇒   dM/dt = γM × B
Summing moments preserves the equation, producing bulk magnetization precession.
