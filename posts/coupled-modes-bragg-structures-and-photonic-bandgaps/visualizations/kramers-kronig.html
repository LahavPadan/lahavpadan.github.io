Causality in the frequency domain

One causal response, two inseparable spectra

The real part χ′ changes phase and refractive index; the imaginary part χ″ describes absorption or gain. Kramers–Kronig says they are two views of the same causal material response—not two curves that may be chosen independently.

In time

The material responds only after the field arrives

That single restriction—no response for t < 0—is what constrains the frequency response.

Why the principal value appears

The real-axis path reaches a pole, so it passes equally on both sides

Causality leaves the upper half-plane free of singularities, so the contour may close there. The Kramers–Kronig kernel still has a pole on the boundary at ω′ = ω. The path therefore makes a symmetric detour around that point.

What the relation forbids

A causal material cannot have an arbitrary refractive-index curve while keeping absorption and gain unchanged everywhere. Alter χ″ somewhere in the spectrum and the weighted reconstruction forces χ′ to change as well; the second Kramers–Kronig equation runs the same constraint in reverse.
