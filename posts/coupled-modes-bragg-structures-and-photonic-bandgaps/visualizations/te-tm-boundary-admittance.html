Angular boundary conditions

Oblique incidence gives TE and TM different boundary mismatches

Normal incidence leaves no preferred transverse direction. Tilting the ray fixes a plane of incidence; the electric field can then be perpendicular to that plane (TE) or lie inside it (TM), and the two choices produce different optical admittances.

Normal incidence

no preferred transverse axis
Both polarizations reduce to the same rule: YTE = YTM = n . Rotating the transverse basis changes only its names, not the reflection strength.
