What the construction says

\(y\) is fixed throughout. Only \(x\) is traversed.

1

At each \(x\), measure the excess

\[ g_y(x)=\langle y,x\rangle-f(x). \]

This varies because \(x\) varies; \(y\) does not.

2

Keep the largest excess

\[ f^*(y)=\sup_x g_y(x). \]

No point of the purple line exceeds \(f\) by more than this amount.

3

Subtract that amount everywhere

\[ \langle y,x\rangle-f^*(y) \le f(x) \qquad\text{for every }x. \]

The green line therefore lies below \(f\) over the entire graph.

4

Why this is the largest intercept

\[ c=-f^*(y). \]

If \(c\) were any larger, the red line would exceed \(f\) where the supremum is attained.

Finite case. The supremum gives one finite downward shift, so a highest finite intercept exists.
\[ f^*(y) = \sup_x \bigl( \langle y,x\rangle-f(x) \bigr) \] \(\Longleftrightarrow\) \[ -f^*(y) = \sup \left\{ c: \langle y,x\rangle+c \le f(x) \ \text{for every }x \right\} \]
