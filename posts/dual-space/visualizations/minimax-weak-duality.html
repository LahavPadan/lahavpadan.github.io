The warm ridge dominates the violet slice at every fixed x. That pointwise comparison is the whole weak-duality argument.
fixed x \(\displaystyle \sup_y F(x,y) \geq F(x,y_0)\) The warm point is the highest point among all slices, including the violet one.
take the lowest x \(\displaystyle \inf_x \sup_y F(x,y) \geq \inf_x F(x,y_0)\) Taking an infimum preserves a pointwise inequality.
let the slice vary \(\displaystyle \inf_x \sup_y F(x,y) \geq \sup_y \inf_x F(x,y)\) Choose the fixed slice whose lowest point is highest.
