the weight is the coordinate stretching

Uniform angles become crowded near t = ±1

The endpoint weight is not an extra rule attached afterward. It records how the cosine projection compresses equal angle intervals.

equal steps in θ → unequal steps in t
|dt| = sin θ dθ
dθ = |dt| / √(1 − t²)
w(t) = 1 / √(1 − t²)
What this section establishes: Tn is an ordinary cosine mode transported from the angle coordinate to the interval. Its nodes and its orthogonality weight both come from that same coordinate change.

what SU(2) contributes

A matrix stores the same two opposite phases

The group-theoretic section does not introduce a different angle. SU(2) is the collection of 2 × 2 complex matrices that preserve length and have determinant 1. After diagonalization, one such matrix packages the opposite phases eⁱᶿ and e⁻ⁱᶿ.

power first, then add the diagonal

The half-trace is exactly the first-kind Chebyshev polynomial

Raising the matrix to the n-th power multiplies both angles by n. Taking the trace adds the two resulting phases.

paired phases
Dθ = diag(eiθ, e−iθ)
→

→

½ tr(Dθ4) = cos(4θ)
x = ½ tr(Dθ) = cos θ,    T4(x) = ½ tr(Dθ4) = cos(4θ)

So Tn reads how the trace changes when the same matrix is raised to the n-th power.

03

why the second kind also appears

The n-dimensional trace adds a whole ladder of phases

On degree n − 1 monomials, the same diagonal matrix contributes the evenly spaced exponents n − 1, n − 3, …, −(n − 1). Their sum is the second-kind polynomial.

the diagonal entries being added

for dimension n = 4, the exponents are 3, 1, −1, −3

χ₄(θ) = e3iθ + eiθ + e−iθ + e−3iθ = sin(4θ) / sin θ = U₃(cos θ)

T₄(cos θ) = cos(4θ)

second kind

The full trace of the corresponding 4-dimensional action.
