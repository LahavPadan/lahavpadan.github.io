Near a Bragg condition, two waves that were previously distinct become strongly coupled. The result is a pair of allowed frequencies separated by a forbidden interval.

A minimal model has the form

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
\delta & \kappa \\
\kappa & -\delta
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix} A \\ B \end{pmatrix}
= q\begin{pmatrix} A \\ B \end{pmatrix}.
$$

The eigenvalues are

$$
q = \pm\sqrt{\delta^2 + \kappa^2}.
$$

The point is visible immediately: when the uncoupled waves would cross, the coupling $\kappa$ prevents the crossing and creates a gap.
