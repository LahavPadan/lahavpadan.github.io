Singular cubic, one line parameter

Why the slope of a line determines one point on a singular cubic

Every green line has equation y = tx, so it passes through the marked point S = (0,0). Substituting into the curve turns the intersection with the line into a cubic equation in x, whose roots are the x-coordinates of the intersection points. On a node or cusp, that cubic factors as x² × (linear in x): the x² consumes two of the three roots at S, so exactly one root — and one other intersection point — remains. A smooth cubic has no repeated factor, so the same substitution leaves two other roots and a slope through S does not single out one point.
