The chord-and-tangent construction

A chord determines the third intersection; reflection gives the sum.

The relation on the line

R = −(P + Q)

The third intersection and the sum share an x-coordinate and have opposite y-coordinates.

1Draw the chord.
The line is fixed by P and Q.
2Locate R.
A line and a cubic meet three times, counting multiplicity.
3Reflect R.
The reflected point is P + Q.

Coordinate formula

The tangent case is not a separate rule: when Q approaches P, the chord approaches the tangent, and the same third-intersection-and-reflection construction remains valid.
